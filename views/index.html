PROCESS

Dispatch to Destination
in Four Steps

🚨

Incident Reported

The EMT inputs the injury type and severity directly into OCCO. The severity of the injury determines where you go, from a local urgent care to a hospital that provides specialized care 24/7.

🏥

Hospitals Contacted

OCCO automonously calls any hospitals within range to ask for current ER wait times. These will be taken into account for route selection.

🗺️

Route Calculated

The fastest routes are calculated based on ER times, hospital congestion, live traffic data, and the position of other OCCO units. The user selects the route that is recomended, and the hospital that the user selects is notified, so they can get ready for the patient arrival.

🧭

Navigate & Adapt

The driver follows the GPS directions that update in real time. If a faster route opens up, OCCO prompts a reroute decision to make sure the driver stays focused, not distracted by their phones.

CAPABILITIES

Built for the Demands
of Real Emergency Response

⚡

Fleet-Wide Coordination

As more units join the OCCO network, the system gets smarter. Ambulances share destination data in real time, preventing ER overloads by distributing patients intelligently across all available hospitals. The network effect is compounding — more units means better predictions for every unit.

3.1× better distribution with 10+ units active
🚦

Live Traffic Intelligence

Integrates with open mapping APIs for real-time traffic data. Routes are recalculated continuously — not just at dispatch.

📋

Injury-Matched Facilities

Severity scoring ensures minor injuries go to closer, less burdened clinics, freeing major trauma centers for critical cases.

📞

Automated Hospital Pre-Alert

Hospitals receive the injury type and ETA before the ambulance arrives so staff can prepare the appropriate care environment in advance.

📊

Route History & Analytics

Every route is logged. City administrators get actionable data on response times, hospital usage, and network performances.
